Who are the homeowners?

Choose a State:

Here we explore home ownership along two other dimensions: age and race.

There is ongoing discourse on how young people are unable to afford homes. In the United States overall, 90% of owner-occupied homes are owned by those over the age of 35. A stark contrast from the 1960s, when the average age for a First Time Buyer was 23.

As expected, we see a strong trends between race and homeownership. Exclusionary racial covenants that targeted specifcally African Americans often extended to other minority groups. Despite the supreme court ruling these covenants unconsitutional in 1948 (Shelley v. Kraemer), these clauses can be found in many deeds even today.
